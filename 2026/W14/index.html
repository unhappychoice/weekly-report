Test coverage triples and CI automation takes over github-weekly-reporter

From 40% to 97% coverage, Dependabot integration, and critical date-handling bug fixes in one week

This week was all about the github-weekly-reporter project. I threw myself into improving codebase quality and setting up the automation needed to maintain it. The goal was to get the project ready for public release, and I think we crossed the finish line.

The biggest win was expanding test coverage from a shaky 40% to a solid 97% across two massive PRs. I added 128 new tests and fixed countless edge cases. To keep that coverage from slipping, I integrated Codecov and set up Dependabot to handle dependency updates across all my repositories. Meanwhile, I squashed a critical bug in the daily-fetch cron that was losing Sunday's events and another that was pulling the wrong week's data.

With the codebase now thoroughly tested and dependencies automatically updated, I'm confident the project can scale. The next step is to spread the word and get more users generating their own weekly reports.

Fixing date handling in daily-fetch
Two critical bugs in the data collection pipeline were causing missing events. The first bug made daily-fetch collect yesterday's events on Mondays, losing Sunday's data. The second made weekly-fetch target the previous ISO week mid-week, resulting in empty reports. Both are now fixed with timezone-aware date logic.
