Fixing daily-fetch's week range bug and stabilizing the pipeline

A cascade of critical bug fixes, test coverage pushes, and dependency upgrades after discovering the core data collection was targeting the wrong week.

This week was dominated by a critical discovery: the daily-fetch cron job, which runs at midnight, was incorrectly collecting data for the previous ISO week instead of the current one. This meant on Monday nights, Sunday's contributions were permanently lost because the week ID had already rolled over. The fix required a careful rethink of the date range logic and triggered a wave of related corrections.

With the core data pipeline stabilized, the focus shifted to hardening the entire system. This meant improving error handling for API rate limits and permissions, preventing private repo leaks in reports, and ensuring incomplete weekly reports didn't break the index page navigation. In parallel, a major push brought test coverage from 95% to 97%, and I finally got Dependabot configured to automate the endless dependency updates across my entire portfolio.

The week culminated in a massive, focused burst of 283 commits on Saturday (April 4th), where I merged a backlog of dependency updates (Vitest 4, ESLint 10, TypeScript 6) and polished documentation. The rhythm was intense: deep debugging early in the week, systematic quality improvements mid-week, and a final sprint to close the loop on maintenance tasks.

The Great Week Range Mix-up
The daily-fetch cron, firing at midnight Monday, used a function that targeted the previous ISO week. This caused Sunday's events to be skipped entirely when the week rolled over. The fix involved creating new buildCurrentWeekRange and getCurrentWeekId functions to correctly target the ongoing week's data, ensuring no contributions are lost at week boundaries.
