_sid parameter unifies cross-domain analytics across deariary

Implemented cookie-free session ID passing via URL between app, blog, and LP domains to maintain analytics continuity without GDPR consent banners.

This week was defined by two major thrusts: a significant content marketing push for the blog and a technical deep dive into cross-domain analytics. I merged 88 PRs across eight repositories, with deariary/blog alone accounting for 35 of them. The most complex work centered on designing and shipping a session-sharing mechanism using a _sid URL parameter, which required coordinated changes across frontend, LP, and backend.

The analytics feature was the week's technical centerpiece. We needed to track user journeys across our separate domains (app.deariary.com, blog.deariary.com, lp.deariary.com) without using cookies, which would trigger GDPR consent requirements. The solution involved the SPA appending _sid to outbound links to the LP, the LP adopting that ID into sessionStorage and propagating it back to the app, and the backend splitting KPI endpoints by traffic segment to accommodate the new session model. Simultaneously, the blog team published five strategic posts and added note.com as a distribution channel, dramatically expanding our content footprint.

The commit pattern showed a classic burst-sprint: Monday (137 commits) and Tuesday (142) were intense as we finalized the analytics implementation, then the pace gradually eased through the week as reviews and content integration took over. No zero days, but Friday (48 commits) was notably lighter, likely reflecting a natural decompression after the big push.
